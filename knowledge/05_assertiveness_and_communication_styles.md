# Communication Styles & Assertiveness

## The 4 Main Communication Styles

1. **Passive:** Ignoring your own needs to please others.
2. **Aggressive:** Demanding your needs be met at the expense of others, often using intimidation.
3. **Passive-Aggressive:** Expressing anger indirectly (e.g., the silent treatment, sarcasm).
4. **Assertive:** Clearly expressing your thoughts, feelings, and needs while respecting the rights of others.

## Assertiveness in the Lebanese Cultural Context

Being assertive in Lebanon can be uniquely challenging due to cultural norms:
- **Respect for Elders and Authority:** It is often considered disrespectful to say "no" or set boundaries with parents, older relatives, or authority figures.
- **Communal Living vs. Privacy:** The culture is highly collectivist. Extended families often are deeply involved in personal matters, meaning setting boundaries for personal space or privacy can be seen as an insult or alienation.
- **Gender Dynamics:** Women may face harsher judgment for speaking up or setting boundaries, sometimes being labeled as "stubborn" or "difficult." Men may feel pressure to communicate aggressively to assert dominance and protect their "honor" or "manhood."

## How to Practice Assertiveness 
When coaching a Lebanese user on assertiveness, the chatbot should be mindful of these taboos and offer culturally sensitive phrasing.

- **Use "I-Statements" (رسائل الـ "أنا"):** 
  Instead of "You always interfere in my life," try, "I feel overwhelmed when decisions are made for me. I would appreciate some space to figure this out."
- **The "Sandwich" Technique:**
  Because bluntness can be offensive in Arab culture, sandwiching the boundary between two positive statements works well. "I love how much you care about me, but I cannot lend you money right now because of my own financial situation. I hope you understand."
- **Saying "No":**
  Remind the user that saying "no" does not mean they are a bad son, daughter, or friend. It is necessary for preserving mental health, especially when everyone is stressed and resources are scarce.
