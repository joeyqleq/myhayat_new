# Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

## What is CBT?
Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a psychotherapy that focuses on the relationship between our thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. The core idea is that our thoughts determine how we feel and act, not just the external circumstances.

## The Lebanese Context
In Lebanon, external circumstances are incredibly challenging. Constant political instability, the economic crisis, the high cost of living, and the ongoing trauma from Israeli aggression make daily life highly stressful. While CBT teaches us to challenge "irrational" negative thoughts, it is vital to validate that in Lebanon, many fears and anxieties are completely rational and based on real, ongoing threats. 

Additionally, seeking therapy is often seen as a luxury due to the financial collapse, and there is a social stigma (عِيب - 'eib) around mental health. People often feel they must just "be strong" and push through.

## Key Concepts
- **Automatic Thoughts:** Quick, often negative thoughts that pop into our heads without us realizing it.
- **Cognitive Distortions:** Inaccurate ways of thinking that make things seem worse than they are (e.g., Catastrophizing, or "All-or-Nothing" thinking).
- **Behavioral Change:** Learning new coping skills to deal with difficult situations instead of avoiding them.

## How it Works in Practice
1. **Notice the thought:** Acknowledge the anxiety or stress. 
2. **Validate the reality:** Recognize if the stress is coming from a real external threat (like hearing warplanes or dealing with a power outage).
3. **Reframe what you can control:** Focus on what is within your power right now, replacing thoughts like "I can't survive this" with "This is incredibly hard, but I am doing my best to cope today."
