# Bipolar Disorder

## What is Bipolar Disorder?
Bipolar Disorder is a mental health condition that causes extreme mood swings. These include emotional highs (Mania or Hypomania) and deep lows (Depression). 

## The Stigma in Lebanon
In Lebanese culture, seeking psychiatric help carries a heavy social stigma (عِيب). Mental illness is often misunderstood as a sign of weak faith, a character flaw, or "craziness" (جنوون). 
- **For Men:** The societal expectation is to be the strong, stoic provider. Admitting to struggling with bipolar disorder, especially depressive episodes, can feel like a failure of masculinity. Men often hesitate to seek help until a crisis occurs.
- **For Women:** There is fear that a diagnosis will affect their marriage prospects, their role as mothers, or bring "shame" to the family name. 

Because of this, many people suffer in silence or try to hide their symptoms, which only exacerbates the condition.

## The Economic and Security Reality
Managing Bipolar Disorder requires stability, routine, and often medication. In Lebanon today, these are luxuries:
- **Medication Shortages:** The economic collapse has made psychiatric medications extremely expensive, and sometimes they simply aren't available in pharmacies.
- **High Stress:** The constant threat of war, Israeli aggression, and financial ruin are massive triggers for both manic and depressive episodes.
- **Cost of Therapy:** Private psychiatrists and therapists are unaffordable for the vast majority of the population.

## Coping Strategies When Help is Hard to Get
The chatbot should focus on harm reduction and free coping mechanisms:
1. **Sleep Hygiene:** A strict sleep schedule is the most important non-medical defense against manic episodes.
2. **Limit News Consumption:** Doomscrolling the news about war and the economy is a major trigger. Advise setting strict limits (e.g., checking the news only once a day).
3. **Identify Triggers and Early Warning Signs:** Help the user recognize the signs that their mood is shifting so they can take preventative action (like isolating less during a depressive shift, or handing over financial control to a trusted family member during a manic shift).
4. **Lean on Trusted Friends:** Since professional help is expensive, encourage the user to build a small, trusted support system of people who understand their condition and won't judge them.
