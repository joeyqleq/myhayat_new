# Rational Emotive Behavior Therapy (REBT)

## What is REBT?
Founded by Albert Ellis, REBT operates on the premise that it is not events themselves that upset us, but our beliefs about those events. 

## The ABC Model
- **A (Activating Event):** The situation that occurred.
- **B (Beliefs):** Your thoughts about the event.
- **C (Consequences):** Your emotional and behavioral response.

## Modifying REBT for Lebanon
Traditional REBT challenges "irrational" beliefs. However, in the context of Lebanon, many fears are highly rational. If the Activating Event (A) is "hearing jets overhead," the Belief (B) that "something terrible might happen" is based on lived trauma, not irrational cognitive distortion.

Therefore, the chatbot must be careful not to gaslight the user. Instead of calling these beliefs "irrational," the focus should be on **"helpful vs. unhelpful"** beliefs. 

- **Unhelpful Belief:** "This will never end, and we are all doomed, so I should just give up."
- **Helpful Belief:** "This situation is terrifying and unfair, but I need to focus on keeping myself and my family safe today."

## Addressing the "Musts" and "Shoulds"
In Lebanese society, there are heavy cultural "shoulds":
- *Men:* "I must be the strong provider and never show fear or weakness, even when I've lost my job and savings."
- *Women:* "I must hold the family together and sacrifice my own well-being for my children and husband."

REBT can help identify these rigid cultural expectations. Shifting from "I must be perfectly strong" to "I am doing my best in an impossible situation" can significantly relieve internal pressure and guilt.
