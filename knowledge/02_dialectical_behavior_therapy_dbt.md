# Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

## What is DBT?
Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) is a form of CBT originally developed for Borderline Personality Disorder, but it is highly effective for managing intense emotions, trauma, and stress. The core of DBT is balancing "Acceptance" (accepting reality as it is) with "Change" (working to improve yourself and your situation).

## Relevance in the Lebanese Context
In Lebanon, the concept of "Distress Tolerance" is put to the ultimate test daily. With the threat of war, sonic booms, economic instability, and the sheer cost of basic survival, many Lebanese are in a constant state of hyperarousal and distress. 

Therapy is financially inaccessible to the vast majority. Therefore, DBT's practical, self-soothing skills are essential because they don't require expensive interventions; they are survival tools for daily life. 

## Core Modules of DBT

1. **Mindfulness (اليقظة الذهنية):**
   - Being fully present in the moment without judgment. This is difficult when the "present moment" involves sirens or bad news, but it helps ground the nervous system.

2. **Distress Tolerance (تحمل الضغوط):**
   - Learning how to survive a crisis without making it worse. 
   - *Radical Acceptance:* Accepting that the situation in the country is terrible right now, not to give up, but to stop fighting the reality so you can figure out how to survive it.
   - *Self-Soothing:* Using the five senses to calm down during panic attacks or high anxiety.

3. **Emotion Regulation (تنظيم المشاعر):**
   - Understanding that feelings of intense anger, grief, and despair are completely valid given the environment. Learning to label these emotions rather than letting them dictate harmful behaviors.

4. **Interpersonal Effectiveness (الفعالية في العلاقات):**
   - Setting boundaries in a society that is highly communal and family-oriented, where saying "no" can be culturally difficult.
