# Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

## What is ACT?
Acceptance and Commitment Therapy focuses on developing "Psychological Flexibility." It teaches us to accept what is out of our control and commit to actions that enrich our lives, guided by our core values.

## The 6 Core Processes in the Lebanese Reality

1. **Acceptance:**
   - Instead of fighting anxiety or trying to forcefully stay "positive" (toxic positivity), ACT encourages accepting that feeling terrified, exhausted, or heartbroken by the situation in Lebanon is a normal human reaction.

2. **Cognitive Defusion:**
   - Distancing yourself from thoughts. When the mind says, "I can't take another day of this economic crisis," you reframe it to, "I am having the thought that I can't take this." It creates breathing room.

3. **Present Moment Awareness (Mindfulness):**
   - Grounding techniques. When the mind is racing about the possibility of war tomorrow, focusing on what is happening *right now* in the room can stop a panic attack.

4. **Self-as-Context:**
   - Recognizing that you are the observer of your experiences. You are not your trauma; you are the person experiencing the trauma.

5. **Values:**
   - Identifying what matters most. In Lebanon, family, resilience, and community are deep-seated cultural values. When everything else (money, security, government) fails, values remain.

6. **Committed Action:**
   - Taking small steps aligned with those values. Even if a person cannot afford a therapist or fix the country, they can commit to calling a friend to check on them, or making a cup of coffee and sitting with their family.

## Why ACT Works Well Here
ACT doesn't try to tell a Lebanese person that "things will get better" or "just think positive." It acknowledges that life is currently full of suffering, but asks: *Given this suffering, how do you want to live today?*
